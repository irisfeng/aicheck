## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are internal cybersecurity reviewers, onboarding coordinators, and implementation staff who need to decide whether a customer can pass network and information security review before integration and launch. Secondary users are customer-side operators who upload evidence materials and need clear feedback on what is missing or non-compliant.

### Brand Personality
Professional, restrained, trustworthy.

The product should feel calm and efficient rather than flashy. It should reduce reviewer fatigue, increase confidence in each conclusion, and make evidence traceability obvious at a glance.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first editorial minimalism with subtle industrial precision.

Use a refined, evidence-first interface: warm neutral backgrounds, ink-toned text, muted teal or deep green accents for compliant states, and restrained amber or brick accents for risk and blocking issues. Avoid neon, cyberpunk, dashboard clutter, generic card walls, and purple/blue AI gradients. The UI should feel like a high-end review workbench instead of a generic AI console.

### Design Principles
1. Evidence first: every conclusion must point to the exact screenshot, page, paragraph, or OCR span that supports it.
2. Progressive disclosure: show pass or fail at a glance, then reveal reasoning, confidence, and raw evidence only when needed.
3. Human override is native: reviewers can amend judgments without fighting the system.
4. Risk hierarchy beats data density: mandatory blocking items must dominate the visual hierarchy.
5. Calm throughput: reviewers should be able to process many cases in sequence without visual fatigue.
